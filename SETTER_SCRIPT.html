FI Questions — In Order
1. "Are you the primary on the mortgage, or is there someone else on the loan with you?"
2. "Full name — first and last?"
3. "Date of birth?"
4. "Smoker or non-smoker?"
5. "Height and weight?"
6. "Any major health issues — heart, diabetes, cancer, anything like that?"
7. "Any current medications?"
8. "What do you do for work — and roughly what are you pulling in per month from that?"
9. "About how much do you still owe on the mortgage?"
10. "Was that a 15, 20, or 30-year loan?"
11. "What's the monthly payment?"
12. "If something happened to you, [NAME] — who would be responsible for the mortgage?"
13. "On a scale of 1–10 — how important is it that the home is protected for them?"
→ 7 or above → go to commit gate and hand off. Below 7 → "Walk me through what that looks like for them if nothing's in place."
→ After the 1–10 answer — pause. Don't fill the silence. Let it land.

③ Gate
do not pass without this
⚠ Commit Gate — Required Before Handoff
"So let me ask you — would you say getting something in place for [BENEFICIARY] is more of a necessity right now, or more of a nice-to-have?"
Ask it. Then stop talking. First person to speak after this question loses the moment — don't fill the silence.
Necessity → proceed to handoff.
Hedging → "Walk me through what that looks like for [beneficiary] if nothing's in place." Dig back into the why.
Do not present options or hand off until they've verbally committed.
④ Handoff
appointment + button-up
Pre-Handoff Bridge
"Okay — based on what you've shared with me, here's what I'm going to do."
"What I'm going to do is get [NAME] on — he's the senior underwriter on my team and he's the one who actually has access to run the full options for your profile."
"It's a 10-minute call. He'll go over 2 or 3 options, show you the numbers, you decide from there."
"No pressure — you're not committing to anything. You're just getting the full picture."
Book the Time
"Mornings, afternoons, or evenings — which works better for you?"
Let them pick. Then give exactly two specific slots — never "when are you free?"
"I have [TIME 1] or [TIME 2] — which one works?"
"And will [SPOUSE] be there as well? I want to make sure you both have the full picture — this is the most important plan your family will have."
If spouse won't be there → don't book. "What time works where you're both home? I don't want either of you to miss this."

If they give a reason → handle now. Rebook on the spot before hanging up.
