Phase 1 · Aged Mortgage
Hook
You manage the brokerage. You are the authority. Act like it from the first word.
🎯 Framework — Tonality — Hook Phase Opener: Calm + Warm. 110% volume, 70% speed. Smile while you dial — it comes through the phone. You are a busy expert doing a courtesy call.
Not Interested: Slightly assertive, mildly confused — like you're surprised they'd push back on something this obvious. Never defensive. Never apologetic.
Already Has Coverage: Administrative and warm. You expected this. It's exactly why you called.

Phase 2 · Process Framing
Intro
You are the doctor. They are the patient. Establish structure from the first word. Front-load friction points here so they don't block the close later.
🎯 Framework — Front-Loading (Part 7) Bring up potential friction points early so they don't ambush the close. The 3 Required Pieces section plants the SSN and banking ask right now — so when you ask for it in Tab ⑦ it feels like the logical next step, not a surprise demand.
First Time or Been Through This Before?
"Okay — is this your first time going through the mortgage protection process, or have you been through this before?"
"It's pretty simple. I'm going to spend about two minutes asking you some health and financial questions. Based on that, my job as the medical underwriter is to run everything through the carriers in [State] and figure out what options you qualify for."
"From there, I'll present you the top options, you tell me what's comfortable and affordable, and we submit a request for coverage. Make sense?"
(Wait for response.)
The 3 Types of Insurance on a Home
"So when you close on a home, there are three types of insurance that come into play:"
"Number one — Private Mortgage Insurance, or PMI. Required by the bank if you put less than 20% down. Does absolutely nothing for you or your family — it only protects the bank."
"Number two — Homeowners insurance. Protects the physical structure — fire, flood, storm damage."
"Number three — and the most important one — Mortgage Protection. This is the coverage that protects you and your family if you become sick, disabled, or pass away. It's what ensures your family can stay in the home no matter what."
"Any questions on that?"
3 Required Pieces — Plant the Compliance Frame Now
"Now — mortgage protection isn't something you can just sign up for anytime like car insurance. You have to qualify for it. So once we go to submit a request, there are three pieces of information required:"
"First — driver's license. Confirms your identity. Do you have that on hand?"
(Wait for response.)
"Second — your Social. That's how they check your prescriptions and medical history through the MIB — the Medical Information Bureau. It's the primary factor in whether you're approved or declined."
"Third — either a bank statement or voided check. Confirms there's no prior insurance fraud or money laundering linked to the account — and that's how the premium gets paid once you're approved."
"Make sense?"
🎯 Framework — SSN Front-Load (Part 7) Plant this casually right after the 3 Required Pieces. One line. Said once. This kills the SSN objection before it ever exists.
"Some people get a little jumpy about the Social — totally normal. Just so you know, that's standard on every insurance application. It's how they pull your medical records and verify your identity. Nothing unusual about it."
Intro complete → Tab ③ Financial Inventory
Phase 3 · Data Gathering
Financial Inventory
Slow the pace all the way down. Administrative and intentional — not an interrogation. Everything they tell you is ammunition for Building the Why. Work through this in order — every field is a weapon you load now and fire in Tab ④.
🎯 Framework — Ammunition (Part 1 + 5) Gather ALL facts before you pitch anything. No mediocre answers — if they give you something surface-level, push back. Map every asset gap before the emotional close. The deeper your inventory, the easier your Why-build.
Personal Info — Primary
"Just to confirm — you're still at [Address]?"
"Full name — first and last?"
"And date of birth?"
"What do you do for work? And roughly what are you pulling in per month from that?"
(Let them talk. Build rapport. Match their energy — slow for slow, fast for fast. Note: income sources matter — W-2, 1099, pension, rental? If self-employed, flag income reliability for underwriting.)
"Smoker or non-smoker?"
"Go ahead and verify your height and weight for me."
"Any surgeries in the past few years?"
"Any current medications?"
(Note medications — look up drug class if unfamiliar. Medications reveal conditions they may not volunteer.)
"Any DUIs, felonies, or anything on your background that might come up?"
(Frame casually — administrative, not accusatory. Some carriers decline or rate up on DUI history.)
"And any debt outside of the mortgage — car payments, credit cards, anything significant?"
Spouse / Co-Applicant Info
"Are you married, single, or somewhere in between?"
(If spouse: get name, age, occupation, income, smoker status, height/weight, medications, surgeries, background. Never close without both decision-makers present.)
(If spouse not available: "What time works best to get [Spouse] on as well? I want to make sure you both have the full picture.")
Household Income & Survivorship
"What are you both pulling in combined per month right now — total household?"
"And who are the dependents in the home — kids, family members living with you?"
"If something happened to you — what would [Spouse / Beneficiary] realistically bring in on their own? Any pension, Social Security, rental income — anything they'd fall back on?"
(This is the survivorship question. Note the gap between household income and what survives. That gap is what you're covering.)
"And what's your target retirement age?"
Medical Conditions — Full Screen
📋 Product Routing by Health Profile Healthy — no major conditions → Term or IUL  ·  Mild — controlled BP, Type 2 diabetes → Term/IUL, check underwriting  ·  Serious — multiple meds, prior heart/cancer/COPD/stroke → Whole Life / Equity Protection  ·  Multiple serious conditions → Guaranteed Issue
"Let me run through the standard medical questions real quick — this is what determines which carriers you qualify for."
"Any history of heart attack, stroke, or TIA?"
"Any cancer — ever?"
"Any stents or heart procedures?"
"Diabetes — pills or insulin?"
(If diabetes: Type 1 or 2? Last A1C? Insulin-dependent? Controlled?)
"High blood pressure — on medication for that?"
"Any neuropathy, lupus, or rheumatoid arthritis?"
"Asthma or COPD?"
(If COPD: albuterol vs. corticosteroid? Corticosteroid = more serious, may route to GI.)
"Any thyroid issues?"
"Any anxiety or depression — on medication?"
"Any kidney or liver disease?"
"Any overnight hospitalizations in the past two years?"
"Anything else a doctor has treated you for that an insurance company might flag?"
Mortgage & Property Info
"About how much do you still owe on the mortgage?"
"Was that a 15, 20, or 30-year loan? And was it a refinance or original purchase?"
"What's the monthly payment right now?"
"And if you were to sell the home today — what do you think it would go for?"
(Calculate equity: home value minus balance. Write it down — you'll use this in Equity Protection if applicable.)
"Is this your forever home, or do you see yourselves moving at some point?"
(If NO — moving: "How long do you plan to stay?" This determines coverage term window.)
"Are you making any extra payments to pay down the loan early?"
Asset Inventory — Retirement Golden Question
🎯 Retirement Golden Question This is the most important financial question in the inventory. It forces them to quantify what actually exists to cover the mortgage if they're gone — before you've pitched anything. Every answer is ammunition.
"Let me ask you the big one — what do you actually have right now that could offset the cost of the mortgage if you were to pass away? What would [Beneficiary] use?"
(Let them answer fully. Don't interrupt. Common answers: 401k, savings, family help. All have gaps you'll expose in Tab ④.)
"How many months of bills do you have saved up right now — just as a cushion?"
(This is the runway question. If they say 3 months — that's exactly how long [Beneficiary] has before the house is at risk. You'll feed this back to them in the Why-build.)
"Do you have anything in a 401k, IRA, stocks, bonds, mutual funds, CDs, or significant savings?"
(If 401k/IRA: "I'm assuming that's more of a retirement account — not something [Beneficiary] could immediately liquidate to cover the mortgage?")
(If significant savings: "And how long could that realistically cover the mortgage payments before it ran out?")
Existing Life Insurance
"Do you have any life insurance in place right now?"
(If YES:)
"Is that private or through work?"
"Which company?"
"What's the face amount — how much would pay out?"
"What are you paying per month for that?"
"Who's the beneficiary on that policy?"
"And when did you get that?"
(Note: year purchased + face amount + monthly cost = whether they're overpriced, underinsured, or missing living benefits. All three are flip opportunities.)
(If work insurance: flag now — "I'll come back to that." Policy flip handled in Tab ⑥.)
Health Bridge — Lock In Before the Hold
🎯 Benefit Bridge — Healthy Profiles Only After health questions confirm a clean profile, reward them immediately. Creates anticipation, locks in urgency. Do NOT skip this — the script used to jump straight to the hold without this moment.
(If clean health profile — no major conditions, not heavily medicated:)
"Okay — good news. Based on what you're telling me, you're going to qualify for the top-tier options. That means the best rates, the best carriers, and full living benefits. That's what I was hoping to see."
(If mixed health — some conditions but manageable:)
"Okay — there are definitely some things to work around here, but I've placed a lot of clients in similar situations. I'll make sure I'm matching you to a carrier that's built for your profile."
(If serious health — multiple conditions, routing to WL/GI:)
"Okay — based on what you're describing, standard term probably isn't the right fit. But there are carriers specifically designed for situations like yours, and I'd rather get you in the right product than put you in something that won't pay out when it matters."
Inventory complete → Tab ④ Build the Why Existing policy flagged → Handle in Tab ⑥ Policy Flip after Why-build
Phase 4 · Emotional Anchor
Build the Why
Slow to 40% speed. Listen 80%, talk 20%. This is where the sale is made or lost. Silence is a weapon — use it.
🎯 Framework — Discovery + Commitment Check (Parts 1, 5, 6) No mediocre answers. When they give a surface-level response — dig. The client MUST verbally commit to needing the solution BEFORE you present any numbers. Do not move to the hold until you have that verbal commitment. Walk them from Point A (current state) to Point B (desired future state) — make the cost of doing nothing undeniable.
NEPQ Opening — Situation → Problem → Consequence
"Okay [Client Name] — before we go any further, I'm here to help figure out some form of protection for this home. This is your biggest asset, and we want to make sure it's protected."
"Correct me if I'm wrong — but looking at the situation, God forbid something happened to you yesterday… it sounds like that would put [Beneficiary] in a pretty tough financial spot with the home. Would that be a fair assumption?"
(Statement, not a question. Pause and let them respond.)
"Let me ask you — if something were to happen to you unexpectedly… what actually happens to the mortgage? Who's responsible for it?"
(Listen fully. If they say "they'd figure it out":)
"When you say 'figure it out' — what do you mean exactly?"
"If [Beneficiary] had to cover that mortgage on their own — how long could they realistically keep up with the payments?"
"And if they couldn't keep up — what would happen to the house?"
(Pause. Let it land. Don't rush past this moment.)
"On a scale of 1 to 10 — how concerned does that make you?"
Dig Deep — When They Give Surface Answers
→ "It would be tough / difficult"
"In what way? What would that look like for your family day-to-day?"
→ "They'd sell the home"
"Is that something you'd actually want them to do? What would that do to the family — having to leave?"
→ "Family would help"
"That's great you have family like that. But — would you rather leave them with a check… or a bill?"
(Pause. Let it land.)
→ "I have savings"
"How long could you realistically live off savings? And once that runs out — what happens next?"
→ No emotional event / nothing recent
"Got it — so this isn't coming from something that just happened. It's more about being responsible long-term?"
"What made now the right time to at least look into this?"
→ They say "probably" to anything
"Probably?"
(Just repeat the word back. Pause.)
"So you're not 100% positive?"
(Forces them to either commit or reveal the real hesitation. One of the most powerful follow-ups in the playbook.)
Cancer Analogy — Term / IUL Profiles Only SLOW DOWN
⚡ Only Use for Term or IUL Profiles Skip this section for Whole Life / Equity Protection profiles. Go straight to Equity Protection below.
"Let me put something into perspective, [Client Name]."
"Statistically — 1 in 3 people get cancer at some point in their life. And in 2024, the average cancer treatment runs anywhere from $100,000 to $300,000 in medical bills."
"Let's say — God forbid — you get diagnosed and you're going through treatment. Your income drops to zero because you can't work."
"Now you're trying to cover [monthly expenses] in bills… on top of staring at $120,000 in medical bills."
"God forbid that happened — what would that look like financially for you and your family?"
(Long pause. Let them answer fully. Don't interrupt.)
"Mhmmm… got it… understood."
(More pause. Let the weight of their answer sit.)
The 89% Stat — Deploy After Cancer Analogy or Living Benefits Pitch
🎯 When to Use Drop this after the cancer analogy OR whenever they say "we have life insurance" or "I'm not worried about dying." It reframes the whole conversation from death coverage to living coverage — where your product wins.
"Here's something most people don't realize — the number one cause of bankruptcies and home foreclosures in this country is not death. It's unforeseen medical bills, or becoming disabled and not being able to work."
"You actually have an 89% higher likelihood of becoming sick or disabled than you do of passing away."
(Pause. Let that number land.)
"That's the reason our clients love this coverage — it doesn't just protect the family when you're gone. It protects the home while you're still here."
Probate Education — Use When Urgency Is Soft
🎯 When to Use When they're emotionally detached or downplaying urgency — "family would figure it out," "it'd be fine." Don't wait for them to ask. Drop this proactively. Makes the consequence timeline undeniable.
"Let me explain something most families don't realize — and this is important."
"When someone passes away, if money or property isn't set up properly — everything has to go through what's called probate court. A judge steps in and oversees who gets what, what debts get paid, how everything gets distributed."
"That process can take six months… sometimes twelve… sometimes longer depending on the state."
"During that entire time — the mortgage payment doesn't stop."
(Pause. Hard pause.)
"The bank still expects their money. So if the house is tied up in probate and payments aren't being made — what usually happens to the home?"
(Let them answer. They'll say foreclosure, sell it, lose it. That's the pain. Now you tie it back to the why.)
Healthy Person's Window — Use When They're Stalling Despite Good Health
🎯 When to Use When a healthy lead is dragging their feet — "let me think about it," "maybe later," "I'm fine for now." Health is not a reason to wait. It's the reason to act. Flip the frame.
"When you apply for coverage, you're really getting two things. First — the policy itself. And second — your medical approval. That second piece is actually the most important part."
"Right now, you're in what we call the healthy person's window. Windows open and they close. You're still healthy enough to qualify for preferred coverage — and that window gets harder to qualify for every single year."
(Pause.)
"Here's the thing — if you get approved today and something happens tomorrow, it doesn't matter. They cannot take your medical approval away from you. Your rate and your benefit are locked in from day one."
"Most people who end up paying double for coverage were healthy when they first thought about it. Does that make sense?"
Equity Protection Pitch — Whole Life Profiles Only
⚡ Only Use for Whole Life Profiles Use when health profile requires Whole Life. Skip entirely for Term/IUL profiles.
"As I'm sure you know, [Client Name] — covering the entire mortgage would be close to another full mortgage payment. Which none of my clients in your situation do."
"What my clients do instead is put together what we call an equity protection plan. Has anyone walked you through how equity protection works?"
"Basically it's a much more practical, much more affordable approach. My goal is to solve the maximum amount of your needs with the least amount of insurance necessary."
"And here's something most people don't realize about these whole life products — this isn't just a death benefit. If you were to get diagnosed with a terminal illness, or become critically ill — cancer, heart attack, stroke — this policy can pay out a significant portion of the benefit while you're still alive. So you're protected on both sides: while you're living and after you're gone."
(Pause. Let that land. For aged MP leads with health issues, living benefits on WL is often the strongest value statement — they've lived it or know someone who has.)
"God forbid something happened to you yesterday — who would the home go to today?"
"Would they plan on keeping the home or selling it?"
"Do they live with you or do they have their own place? Local or out of state?"
(Based on answers, determine the coverage window: typically 9 months to 24 months of mortgage payments.)
"For your situation — the most coverage you'd really need is around a year to two years of mortgage payments. That gives [Beneficiary] the time to grieve, sort the finances, and move forward in a healthy way — without being forced into a fire sale on the home."
"The main goal is to protect the [equity amount] you've built in this home and give [Beneficiary] the time to make the right decision without pressure."
Commitment Check — Framework Gate — Do Not Skip
🎯 Framework — Verbal Commitment Gate The client MUST commit to needing the solution before you present numbers. Do not move to the hold without this. If they won't commit here — dig back in before moving forward.
"Based on everything you've shared — it sounds like you want to make sure [Beneficiary] doesn't lose the house no matter what happens. And if something happened while you were still alive — sickness, disability — you'd want the mortgage covered too. Is that fair?"
(Wait for yes.)
"Would you say getting something in place for [Beneficiary] is more of a necessity than a luxury?"
(If they say "luxury" — ask why. Dig back in before moving on.)
(Once they commit: "Perfect.")
"I'm going to put you on a brief 2-to-3 minute hold while I run your information through my system and find the best options available to you. Keep the phone close in case any questions come up. Any questions before I do that?"

Phase 5 · Presentation
Presenting Options
All selling is done. You are a doctor delivering a prescription. Present highest first. Have them write every option down. Silent close — first person to speak loses.
🎯 Framework — Pitching (Part 1) Only offer solutions AFTER establishing value and confirming the verbal commitment in Tab ④. Never pitch early. Present yourself as matching the client to a carrier based on their health and budget — not selling them something.
📋 Before You Present Have them write down: your name, license number, and direct number. Say: "It's very important that [Beneficiary] has my info — God forbid something happens, I can get those funds to your family within 2–4 business days."
Setup — All Product Types
"Okay [Client Name] — are you still with me? Do you still have that pen and paper?"
"I'm going to give you three options. You let me know what's comfortable and affordable, and we'll go from there."
"And just so you know — once we get approved, I'm assigned to you going forward. A year from now I'll check back in. If you want to increase coverage — we do that. If things are tighter and you need to bring it down — we do that too. My job is to make sure you're always in the best position. Make sense?"
Term Life — Healthy Profile (Ages 18–70)
"All three options are with [Carrier] — A-rated, over 100 years in business, never missed a payout."
"What makes these different from standard life insurance is the living benefits. If you were to become disabled, develop a critical illness — stroke, heart attack, cancer — or receive a terminal diagnosis, this policy pays out up to 80% of the face amount while you're still alive."
"That's the number one reason people get this — the number one cause of foreclosures in this country isn't death. It's unforeseen medical bills and being unable to work. You have an 89% higher likelihood of becoming sick or disabled than passing away — that's what this covers."
"Price never goes up. Coverage never goes down. Locked in from day one."
"Option 1 — [100% / full mortgage] — [premium]/month."
"Option 2 — [75% of mortgage] — [premium]/month."
"Option 3 — [50% of mortgage] — [premium]/month."
"Now — I'm the initial underwriter, not the final decision. That's up to [Carrier]. But if I'm able to get you approved — which one of those would make the most sense for you and your family?"
(Silent close. Stop talking. Do not fill the silence.)

"All three are with [Carrier] — structured as an Indexed Universal Life."
"This is a dual-purpose product. It covers the mortgage now — and when the mortgage is paid off, it converts into permanent life insurance with cash value you can access tax-free."
"Full living benefits — up to 80% payout if disabled, critically ill, or terminally ill."
"Cash value grows alongside the market — averaging 5 to 7% per year. Floor-and-cap structure — only upside, no downside risk."
"Price never goes up. Coverage never goes down. Day-one coverage."
"Option 1 — [100% / full mortgage] — [premium]/month."
"Option 2 — [75%] — [premium]/month."
"Option 3 — [60%] — [premium]/month."
"I'm all about sustainability. Which one of those makes the most financial sense for you?"
(Silent close. Stop talking.)
Whole Life / Equity Protection — Unhealthy Profile

"This policy lasts your entire life — no expiration. Price locked from day one, never goes up. Builds cash value at 2 to 4% per year that you can borrow against tax-free."

Phase 6 · Momentum Protection
Objections
An objection is not a no — it's a request for clarity. Acknowledge → Neutralize → Redirect to their why. Never argue. Never defend. Always move forward with a question.
🎯 Framework — Objection Handling (Part 7) Excellent discovery in Tab ④ makes most objections unnecessary. If you're getting a lot of objections, your Why wasn't deep enough — go back and dig. For objections that do come: front-load early (already done in Tab ②), never argue, always redirect to the emotional pain they already shared with you.
📋 Default Reset Line — Use Before Any Rebuttal "Perfect — that's actually the reason for my call. I see that on your file." Resets authority. Use it liberally.
